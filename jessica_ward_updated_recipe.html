Frybread

Description Ingredients Directions

Description

Frybread is a circular piece of dough that is cooked through frying.

Frybread was invented as a result of Natives being put on reservations. Taken away from their indigenous food sources, Natives depended on the space rations,largely consinsting of flour and oil, given to them by the government.

Today, frybread is an important part of pan-Native American culture and is often the food of choice for large gatherings. It is often eaten as a desert with jam, powered sugar, or honey, or as a savory main course, called an 'Indian Taco'

Ingredients

For 40-50 pieces

  • Flour -
  • Salt- 2 Tablespoons
  • Oil - 1 1/2 cups
  • Sugar - 1 cup
  • Yeast - 1 to 2 Tablespons
  • Water - 2 to 3 cups

Directions

  1. Mix dry ingriedients- flour, salt, sugar, yeast.
  2. Add oil
  3. Mix dough, adding water until consistancy is sticky
  4. Cover and allow to rise for 1 to 2 hours
  5. Heat large amount of oil
  6. Take 1/3 cup of dough and stretch it until it is thin and flat, but has no holes
  7. Place dough in oil.
  9. Flip when oil down side is golden brown
  10. Cook other side until golden brown
  11. Allow bread to cool. Paper towels can be used to sop up excess oil.
